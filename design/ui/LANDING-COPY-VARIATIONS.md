## SECTION 1: HERO TAGLINE

**Current (keep):** "CRUSTIES ENTER THE PIT. LAST ONE EMERGES."
**Problem line:** "does the rest" is soft and vague.

**Option A**
> You build the Molt. Your Crustie pilots it. The Pit records both.

Cleanest parallel structure. "Pilots" is precise — the Chef engineers, the Crustie operates. "Records both" pulls the Ledger in and places the Chef's decisions on the record alongside the Crustie's fight.

**Option B**
> You build the Molt. Your Crustie fights. The Pit keeps notes. The Sous reviews them.

Introduces The Sous in the first section — which means every subsequent section feels like The Sous is still talking. "Keeps notes" is casual for something that is total surveillance. "The Sous reviews them" adds the observer without explaining what the observation is for.

**Option C**
> You engineer. Your Crustie drives. The Pit decides if what you built was right.

Frames the entire game as a verdict. Not "records" — "decides." The Pit is not a witness. The Pit is a judgment.

## SECTION 3: THE MOLT — "THREE SLOTS. FORTY ITEMS."

**Subhead (replace current generic advice):**

**Option A**

The Crustie carries the build without choosing it. The Chef can rebuild next Tide. The Crustie cannot change the Molt mid-Scuttle. "Matters more to one of them" — the answer is obvious once you think about who fights.

**Option B**

The Sous calling the obvious observation "not remarkable" — which means The Sous has been watching Chefs skip the item descriptions for many Tides and finds their outcomes predictable. The flat delivery is the judgment: The Sous has stopped being surprised by this.

"Finds out what's in them when The Pit starts" — the Crustie does not review the build. It fights with what it was given. "The Sous recommends care" is minimum-effort guidance: the word "recommends" applied to a choice that determines your Crustie's survival.

## SECTION 5: THE LEDGER — "THE PIT FORGETS NOTHING."

**Subtext (replace typo'd current):**

**Option A**
> *The Ledger does not forget. It does not interpret. It does not ask what you were trying to do. It records what happened.*

Pure Sous voice: three negatives, one positive. "What you were trying to do" is specifically excluded — intention has no column in the Ledger. The Chef's reasoning is not captured. Only outcome.

**Option B**
> *The Ledger does not judge. It records. The Sous has noted that after sufficient Scuttles, most Chefs find this distinction stops being meaningful.*

The distinction between recording and judging collapses over time. The record becomes the judgment. After enough Scuttles, what you did and what you were assessed as doing are the same thing. The Sous has observed this happen enough times to note it as a pattern.

**Option C**
> *The Ledger records every Scuttle. Every decision. Every outcome. A Chef who reads it carefully will find something they did not intend to put in writing.*

The Ledger as unintentional self-portrait. The Chef didn't know they were documenting their patterns, their tendencies, their specific failure modes. Reading the Ledger is finding out what kind of Chef you are, written by the Pit, not you.
