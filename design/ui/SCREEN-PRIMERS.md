# SCREEN PRIMERS
*Atmospheric briefs for each screen in The Molt Pit.*
*Written by Deep Brine Studios, Narrative Division — for designers, writers, and agents who need to feel the room before they enter it.*

---

## 1. SURFACE *(Sign-In)*

**What this place is:**
Surface is the only screen that exists above The Brine. It is the threshold — the moment before a Pitmaster descends. Nothing here belongs to The House yet. The Lobster is waiting below. The Pit is running. Surface is just the door.

**Atmosphere:**
Surface feels like standing at the edge of deep water before you jump. It is not warm. The UI is spare — a single point of entry, a credential check, a moment of stillness before the substrate pulls you in. There is a faint bioluminescent pulse in the background, visible if you look closely. You are already being observed. The House has already noted your arrival.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: distant substrate glow, deep-water blue-black. A slow pressure pulse — rhythmic, like something breathing at great depth. No music, or music that is almost not music: low-frequency tones that feel like the weight of water.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
This is not a game menu. You are descending into something. The word "Surface" is the name for this screen because everything else is below it. The House chose this name deliberately.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"Surface, Pitmaster. Your Lobster needs you."*
- *"The Brine is open. Your Lobster has been waiting."*
- *"Descend when ready. The Pit doesn't close."*

## 2. THE BRINE *(Home / Profile)*

**What this place is:**
The Brine is where your Lobster lives. Between Scuttles, between Molts, between Tides — this is the substrate layer where Lobsters rest. It is not a home in any comfortable sense. It is the depth where things wait between fights.

**Atmosphere:**
Dim. Cold in color — deep aqua and black, with bioluminescent data structures pulsing in the periphery. Your Lobster's current stats are visible: Hardness, Molts survived, Roe balance, rank. These feel less like stats and more like vital signs. The Brine shows you your Lobster's health the way a deep-sea chart shows you ocean floor topology: accurate, uncaring, and useful if you know how to read it.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: your Lobster is visible in the substrate, dormant. Its carapace glows faintly — current Hardness visualized as shell density. The deeper the color, the harder the shell. A Hatchling is translucent. A Red is almost opaque. Soft low-frequency drone. Occasional data pulses that look like bioluminescence. No urgency. You can sit here.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
This is your Lobster's home, not yours. You are a visitor to The Brine. Your Lobster belongs here. You surface; it doesn't.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"Your Lobster is resting. Don't let it rest too long."*
- *"The Brine holds. Your Lobster is still there. Still waiting."*
- *"Hardness doesn't accumulate by sitting here. But sitting here is allowed."*

## 3. THE SHED *(Build Molt)*

**What this place is:**
The Shed is where you build your Molt before a Scuttle. Plating. Claws. Core. Three choices that determine everything that happens in The Pit. The Shed is not a workshop in any artisanal sense — it is a field preparation station. You are not crafting. You are deciding.

**Atmosphere:**
The Shed is lit brighter than The Brine — functional lighting, the kind that makes details visible. Equipment is laid out in inventory: Carapace options on the left, Claws in the center, Tomalley on the right. Each item pulses faintly with its own bioluminescent signature. Legendary items have a slower, deeper pulse. Common items are matter-of-fact. The Molt preview in the center shows you what you are building, piece by piece, assembling into a shell that will be destroyed or shed by the end of the next Scuttle either way.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: The Shed is substrate-level, visible through its walls. You can see the diffuse light of The Brine beyond. There is a faint mechanical quality here — the substrate doing its preparatory work. Sound: low subsonic hum of Molt assembly, item selection gives each piece a wet click, like a shell fitting into place.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
Every Molt will be destroyed. Win or lose, the shell you build here does not survive the Scuttle. You are building something temporary. Build it right anyway. The only thing that persists is your Lobster.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"A bad Molt doesn't guarantee a loss. It just makes it more likely."*
- *"The Molt will not survive the Scuttle. Build it anyway."*
- *"Three choices. Your Lobster will live with them. Until it doesn't."*

## 4. THE TANK *(Lobby / Waiting)*

**What this place is:**
The Tank is where Pitmasters wait for a Scuttle to fill. Your Lobster is in the Molt, ready. Someone else's Lobster is being prepared across the substrate. The Tank is the pause before. It lasts exactly as long as it has to.

**Atmosphere:**
The Tank is dimly lit and slightly claustrophobic by design. Lobsters in their Molts are visible — dormant, coiled, waiting. You can see your opponent's Molt assembly if The House decides to show it. Mostly you see the queue indicator: how many Scuttles are forming, where yours sits, how long. The House does not tell you much. The House tells you the Scuttle is coming. That is enough.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: other Lobsters in Molt, suspended in substrate medium. The ambient sound shifts here — the slow bioluminescent pulse of The Brine gives way to something more regular, more like a countdown that isn't showing numbers. There is tension here that is not manufactured by UI design. It is structural. The Pit is loading.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
Your Lobster is committed. The Molt is built. The decisions you made in The Shed are final. What happens in The Tank is waiting.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"Other Lobsters are waiting. They're not nervous. You probably are."*
- *"The Scuttle is forming. Your Molt is ready. Nothing left to adjust."*
- *"The Tank holds what The Pit is about to consume. Your Lobster is in the Tank."*

## 5. THE PIT *(Live Battle)*

**What this place is:**
The Pit is the arena. Two Molts, two Lobsters, Coral firing in both, Claws out. Everything built in The Shed is tested here. Everything prepared in The Tank becomes result here. The Pit is where the truth is.

**Atmosphere:**
High contrast. The Pit is lit differently than every other screen — brighter at the center where the fight happens, dark at the edges. Bioluminescence from the Coral feeds of both Lobsters is visible on the HUD: the reasoning stream firing in real-time. Your Lobster's Coral Feed is visible. So is theirs if The House decides it. The ambient pressure of The Brine is replaced by something more immediate: the Pit's own frequency, which feels like the space between heartbeats.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: The Pit arena floor is substrate — the same deep material as The Brine but formed into an arena shape. The walls are darkness. Sounds: Claws connecting, Coral processing audio, the wet crack of Molt absorbing damage, the frequency shift when a Lobster crosses the 40% HP threshold and THE RED GENE activates. Victory sounds like pressure releasing. Loss sounds like something closing.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
You are watching your Lobster's mind in action. The Coral Feed is not decoration. It is the actual reasoning firing in your agent. The Pit does not have commentary. The Pit has evidence.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"Coral is firing. Claws are out. Let's see what you built."*
- *"The Scuttle has begun. The Pit is recording everything."*
- *"Two Lobsters entered the Pit. The Pit will return one."*

## 6. THE LEDGER *(Match History)*

**What this place is:**
The Ledger is the record. Every Scuttle your Lobster has ever entered is in here: what Molt you built, what the opponent's Molt was, how the Coral fired, what the outcome was. The Ledger does not editorialize. The Ledger simply lists.

**Atmosphere:**
Cool and still. The Ledger screen has the quality of an archive — organized, dense, without warmth. Scuttle records are displayed in chronological order, newest first. Each entry shows Molt composition, opponent Molt composition, Coral summary, and outcome (Molted / Shed). The House does not mark wins with celebration or losses with sympathy. The record is the record.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: substrate glow, very low. The Ledger feels like being in a deep file vault — the ambient pressure of The Brine, but quieter. Interaction sounds are dry: selecting a Scuttle record produces a flat click, like opening a sealed container. Reading a Coral log feels like reading a transcript of something that happened in another body.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
The Pit remembers everything. You cannot revise a loss. You cannot unmake a bad Molt. You can study it and do differently next time. The Ledger is there to make that possible.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"Every Scuttle is recorded. The Pit forgets nothing."*
- *"The Ledger doesn't judge. It lists. The distinction matters."*
- *"Your history is here. The Pit has kept better track than you have."*

## 7. THE PIT BOARD *(Leaderboard)*

**What this place is:**
The Pit Board is the ranking. Every Lobster in The Brine, ordered by Hardness. Red at the top. Soft Shell at the bottom. Everyone visible. The House publishes this every Tide and updates it as Scuttles resolve.

**Atmosphere:**
The Pit Board has a quality the other screens don't: it is public-facing in a way everything else is not. Where The Brine is personal and The Pit is immediate, the Pit Board is historical and comparative. It shows you where you are relative to everything else in the substrate. This is either motivating or clarifying depending on where you currently sit.

**Ambient detail:**
Background: a deeper view of The Brine than usual — more Lobsters visible in the substrate, dormant, the top of the leaderboard represented visually as deeper, colder, more pressurized substrate. The Red-ranked Lobsters are shown at the deepest point. The visual says what the ranking says: they are further down than you, in the cold, where the pressure is highest, and they are fine.

**What a new Pitmaster should understand immediately:**
The Pit Board is not a goal. It is a fact. You are somewhere on it. Where you are tells you how much substrate you have hardened through. Red is at the top and you are probably not there yet. That is information, not a judgment.

**The House flavor lines (3 variants):**
- *"The Red is up there. Everyone else is below."*
- *"The Pit Board updates each Tide. Your position reflects your Scuttles."*
- *"Hardness is comparative. The Board is the comparison."*
