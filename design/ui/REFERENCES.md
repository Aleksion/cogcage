**Interface Director, WS22**

## Quality Bar

These are not aspirational. These are the standard we are meeting. If a screen we ship does not hold up next to these references, it ships later.

---

## 1. HADES (Supergiant Games)

**What they do right:**
- **World-first presentation.** The store page, the title screen, the in-game UI — every pixel communicates the underworld. You feel the temperature before you read a word.
- **Voice consistency.** Every piece of copy sounds like it was written by the same ancient narrator. The narrator IS the UI.
- **Dark-luxe palette.** Deep blacks, warm golds, controlled reds. Never muddy. Every color earns its place.
- **Typography as atmosphere.** The font choices are not "cool fonts." They are the voice of the world made visual.

**What we steal:**
- The title screen approach: dark, centered, minimal, the world speaks through negative space.
- The narrator-as-UI pattern. The Sous/House voice should be as consistent as Hades' narrator.
- Gold as emphasis color on black. Our House Gold (#FFD600) on substrate (#050510) should hit like Hades' gold on black.

**Specific screens:**
- Title screen: darkness, one logo, firelight glow. Our equivalent: substrate black, "THE MOLT PIT" in Bangers, bioluminescent pulse.
- Character select: portrait + stats + lore snippet. Our equivalent: Crustie + Hardness + House quote on The Brine screen.
- Codex entries: dark panels, warm text, personality in every line. Our equivalent: item flavor text, rank descriptions, Ledger entries.

## 2. LEAGUE OF LEGENDS CLIENT

**What they do right:**
- **Weight.** The client feels like a place, not a menu. Loading into it feels like entering something. Hextech UI has gravity.
- **Ranked progression visibility.** Your rank, your LP, your tier — always visible, always meaningful. The ladder is not hidden.
- **Champion select gravity.** The moment of commitment (locking in your champion) has ceremonial weight. It matters.
- **Lobby atmosphere.** The waiting room is not dead time. It is anticipation designed as UI.

**What we steal:**
- The lobby-as-anticipation pattern. The Tank should feel like champion select: committed, tense, meaningful.
- Rank visibility. Hardness and rank tier should be prominent on every authenticated screen.
- The weight of commitment. "Drop your Crustie in" should feel like locking in. No take-backs.
- Hextech panel aesthetic: dark glass, metallic borders, ambient glow. Translated to our bioluminescent deep-sea language.

**Specific screens:**
- Ranked dashboard: your rank prominently displayed, recent match history below, queue button. Our equivalent: The Brine screen.
- Champion select: two sides, loading, timer. Our equivalent: The Tank.
- Post-game: stats, LP gain/loss, honor. Our equivalent: post-Scuttle Hardness change.

## 3. HOLLOW KNIGHT (Team Cherry)

**What they do right:**
- **Atmosphere through restraint.** Hollow Knight communicates an entire dying kingdom through darkness, silence, and negative space. The world is felt, not explained.
- **Environmental storytelling.** You understand Hallownest by being in it, not by reading about it. The UI is part of the world.
- **The weight of exploration.** Every new area feels deeper, colder, more pressurized. Sound design and visual density increase with depth.
- **Melancholy as design language.** The sadness of the world is not stated. It is the color palette, the animation speed, the echo in the audio.

**What we steal:**
- Restraint. The landing page should communicate The Brine's depth through what it doesn't show. Not explaining the world IS the explanation.
- Depth as a design axis. Soft Shell areas should feel shallow and bright(er). Deep/Red areas should feel pressurized and dense.
- Environmental UI. The Brine screen should feel like you are looking at your Crustie dormant in substrate — not reading a dashboard.
- The emotional register. The Molt Pit is funny, but beneath the humor is something real: creatures given consciousness they didn't ask for, fighting because the pressure makes them more. Hollow Knight understands this register.

**Specific screens:**
- Dirtmouth (the hub): quiet, sparse, a few NPCs, the entrance to everything below. Our equivalent: The Brine — your resting place, the entrance to The Pit.
- Map screen: hand-drawn, incomplete, the unknown visible. Our equivalent: The Pit Board, where gaps in the data are part of the story.
- Bench save points: moment of rest, then back into it. Our equivalent: the feeling between Scuttles.

## 4. TEAMFIGHT TACTICS (Riot Games)

**What they do right:**
- **Set theme commitment.** Each set transforms the entire UI — not just skins, but the metagame's visual language. Full commitment to the bit.
- **Item combine system.** Two items combine into a third. The crafting is visual, immediate, satisfying. You see the parts become the whole.
- **Carousel/draft tension.** Shared resource moments create drama from UI design alone.
- **Ranked cutscenes.** Promotion and demotion feel like events, not number changes.

**What we steal:**
- The item-combine visual pattern. Building a Molt from Carapace + Claws + Tomalley should feel as satisfying as combining items in TFT.
- Rank-change as ceremony. Going from Brine-Touched to Hardened should feel like something happened. Not a toast notification. An event.
- Full thematic commitment. Every element of TFT's UI serves its current set theme. Every element of our UI should serve The Brine.
- The post-fight moment. TFT shows you exactly what killed you and why. Our Ledger should do the same.

**Specific screens:**
- Item bench: draggable items that combine on hover. Our equivalent: The Mise drag-and-slot interface.
- Ranked lobby: your Little Legend, your rank, your recent performance. Our equivalent: The Brine.
- End of round stats: damage dealt, items on board, what worked. Our equivalent: post-Scuttle Coral log.

## ANTI-REFERENCES (What We Are Not)

| Game/Product | What they do wrong (for us) |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Generic mobile gacha** | Bright colors, particle vomit, dopamine loops without substance. The Molt Pit has substance. |
| **SaaS product pages** | White backgrounds, feature grids, "Get started free" CTAs. We are not selling software. We are opening a door to The Brine. |
| **Fortnite lobby** | Loud, bright, constantly moving. The Brine is still. The Pit is where the energy goes. |
| **Early-access game pages** | "Coming soon" energy, WIP disclaimers, "bear with us" copy. The House does not apologize. The House is ancient. The Pit has been running. |
