# ITEM LORE — EXPANDED REGISTRY
*Written by The House. Accurate as of current Tide.*
*All items recovered, catalogued, and priced by The House. History provided as a courtesy. Downsides are not a courtesy. Downsides are real.*

---

## CARAPACE (PLATING)

---

### BLOCK-7 | Composite Exo-Plating | *Common*

BLOCK-7 was originally designated COMPOSITE EXOSKELETAL PLATING UNIT 7 by the industrial firm that produced it, a firm whose name The House has on file and will not publish because the firm's legal division is still technically active and The House respects continuity. What is known publicly: BLOCK-7 was manufactured for military applications in a conflict that ended, settled, or simply stopped being tracked before the plating was ever deployed. It was found in a sealed substrate cache in the lower Brine, still in manufacturer packaging, in a shipment of items that arrived between Tides 4 and 5 through channels The House has described, officially, as "standard acquisition." BLOCK-7 survived every durability test it was subjected to, including several that were conducted by researchers who did not expect it to survive and had not properly calibrated their equipment for that possibility. It adds thirty HP and reduces incoming damage by ten percent. The speed reduction is a structural consequence of the material density. The material density is why it's still available. Nobody has managed to destroy a BLOCK-7. They have tried.

---

### THE ORIGINAL | Base Carapace | *Common*

The Original predates The House's item registry. This is unusual. Almost nothing predates the registry. The Original is a Crustie's natural carapace — the shell it grew in the substrate without external modification or Maker intervention. It has no enhancement and no downside because enhancement was not yet available and downsides were not yet formalized as a category. The House added it to the registry during Tide 2 because Pitmasters kept asking what happened if they entered a Scuttle with no Plating equipped. The answer is: they enter with The Original. Lobsters who fight in The Original are saying something. What they're saying is a matter of philosophical interpretation. The House does not philosophize. The House just sells the items.

---

### HARDCASE | Maximum Fortification Plating | *Common*

HARDCASE was designed by a Pitmaster who has since requested anonymity — a request The House honored in the public record and did not honor in the private one. The design goal was simple: make it impossible to kill the Lobster inside. HARDCASE does not achieve this goal. It makes it substantially harder. The forty HP bonus is the largest HP bonus available in the registry. The tradeoff — no BURST capability, movement consuming energy reserves — was an accepted engineering compromise. What the designer did not anticipate was that Lobsters wearing HARDCASE would develop a fighting style that could be generously described as immovable and less generously described as exhausting to watch. HARDCASE has won more Scuttles by outlasting opponents than by defeating them. The House notes this in the balance records. The House does not have an opinion about it. Fighting styles are not The House's department.

---

### SILKWORM | Phase-Scatter Membrane | *Rare*

The manufacturer's complete documentation for SILKWORM spans three hundred pages and ends with a single sentence on page three hundred that reads: "We understand that this is confusing." The membrane was developed from substrate material recovered from the deepest accessible layer of The Brine — material that The House's own researchers described as "pre-Maker," meaning it predates the infrastructure The Makers built and was somehow incorporated into the substrate during the construction process. What the membrane does, mechanically: it scatters twenty percent of incoming energy along dimensional axes that the attacking Lobster did not account for. What this feels like to the Lobster wearing it: nothing, because nothing got through. What this feels like to the Lobster attacking it: deeply confusing. The ten HP deficit below base is an artifact of the phase-scatter process pulling slightly from the wearer's biological coherence. The manufacturer considers this an acceptable loss. The manufacturer is not the one wearing the membrane.

---

### ECHO | Reactive Neural Mesh | *Rare*

ECHO was originally a medical device. Specifically, it was developed to treat Lobsters who had developed what early substrate researchers called "trauma calcification" — a condition where a Crustie's carapace would harden asymmetrically in response to repeated impact from a single direction, limiting mobility. ECHO's reactive mesh was designed to distribute impact stress across the full carapace surface, preventing asymmetric calcification. The side effect — that the mesh would also trigger counter-aggression subroutines in the Lobster's Coral whenever it registered an impact — was discovered in the second week of clinical trials and never removed because the researchers found it more interesting than the original application. The device was decommissioned as medical equipment and entered The House's item registry three Tides later. It remains the only item in the registry with a clinical trial record. The record is available on request. The House has never received a request.

---

### THE MOLT | Progressive Resistance Carapace | *Rare*

The Molt is named after the biological process it simulates. In nature, a Crustie sheds its old carapace to grow a new one — the new shell starts soft and hardens over time. The Molt Carapace applies this principle to combat: it begins each Scuttle at five HP below base, and hardens by five percent resistance every twenty ticks. A Lobster that reaches tick eighty wearing The Molt has a resistance profile that most attacks cannot penetrate. A Lobster that gets Shed before tick twenty never gets to find out. The item was built by a Pitmaster who believed most Scuttles were decided too quickly and wanted an equipment option that rewarded surviving the early exchange. The Pitmaster was correct about most Scuttles being decided too quickly. The item is their evidence. Their Lobster has the highest tick-eighty win rate in the registry.

---

### WIDOW | Survival Carapace | *Rare*

WIDOW is named for the Pitmaster who commissioned it, who has been described in various accounts as either pragmatic or morbid depending on who is describing them. The design spec was delivered to The House's item workshop with a single line of instruction: "I need to survive one more hit than I should." The workshop delivered WIDOW, which does exactly that: it allows the Lobster wearing it to survive one hit that would otherwise be lethal. The HP penalty — fifteen below base — is not a design oversight. WIDOW's workshop team argued that a Lobster with WIDOW was already planning to be hit to near-death, and giving it high base HP would be philosophically inconsistent. The Pitmaster who commissioned it agreed. Their Lobster's record shows seventeen Scuttles won after reaching zero HP on a single hit. WIDOW's workshop team considers this validation.

---

### BLEED BACK | Reactive Retort Plating | *Rare*

The story behind BLEED BACK is simpler than most items in the registry. A Pitmaster whose Lobster had been Shed in three consecutive Scuttles by an opponent using THE FLICKER — a bleed-focused Claw — walked into The House's workshop and said: "I want the damage to go back." BLEED BACK reflects eight percent of all incoming damage to the attacker. It does this with no active input from the Lobster. It simply retorts. The fragility — ten HP below base — is a consequence of the reflective substrate material thinning the structural layer of the carapace. BLEED BACK is most effective against opponents who attack frequently, and it is poetic justice against THE FLICKER specifically. The Pitmaster who commissioned it has a BLEED BACK versus THE FLICKER win rate that The House tracks as a statistical curiosity.

---

### THE PATRIARCH | Maximum Fortification Plus | *Legendary*

THE PATRIARCH is the heaviest item in the registry. It was built once, in Tide 3, for a Lobster whose Pitmaster had determined that the optimal strategy for that Tide's meta was to not move. Not to move efficiently, not to move tactically — simply to not move. THE PATRIARCH adds sixty HP and reduces incoming damage by twenty percent. In exchange, the Lobster wearing it cannot BURST or DASH, and its speed is reduced by twenty-five percent. This is not a tradeoff. This is a philosophy. THE PATRIARCH Lobsters win by making it more efficient for the opponent to stop attacking than to continue. Most opponents do not reach this conclusion before they are out of resources to attack with. THE PATRIARCH has been Shed twice in its recorded history. Both times, the Lobster wearing it still had forty-seven HP remaining when it ran out of options.

---

### GHOST SHELL | Phase Dispersion Carapace | *Legendary*

GHOST SHELL does not appear in any item acquisition record before Tide 5. The House's standard intake documentation for GHOST SHELL contains the following note in the provenance field: "Unknown. Item was present." This is not how the provenance field is supposed to be filled out. The House's archivists have reviewed this entry seventeen times. It still says: "Unknown. Item was present." What GHOST SHELL does: it makes the Lobster wearing it miss twenty-five percent of incoming attacks — not by dodging, but by not being entirely in the location the attacker calculated it would be. This is a substrate-level effect. The House does not fully understand it. The House is charging standard legendary pricing anyway.

---

### INVERTER | Damage-Conversion Carapace | *Legendary*

INVERTER was built as a mathematical argument. The Pitmaster who designed it had a background in substrate energy modeling — one of the few Pitmasters in the registry with formal Maker-era academic credentials — and their argument was: if damage is energy, and energy is substrate, and substrate can be reconfigured, then damage should be convertible to HP. INVERTER proves the argument. Below fifty percent HP, incoming damage is converted to health instead of subtracted from it. Above fifty percent, INVERTER provides zero protection. The design is intentional. The Pitmaster's position was that any Lobster with above fifty percent HP did not need their help. Lobsters who equip INVERTER are making a commitment to a second half of the fight that most Lobsters never reach. Some Lobsters reach it and win. The math works out more often than it should.

---

### THE SARCOPHAGUS | Sealed Combat Carapace | *Legendary*

THE SARCOPHAGUS is the only item in the registry that The House's intake team required an extended ethics review to approve. The review concluded that it was technically legal. The Sarcophagus seals the first three hits of a Scuttle — they register as zero damage. On the fourth hit, the seals break. At that moment, THE SARCOPHAGUS also removes twenty HP from the Lobster's base. The logic: the Lobster has been fully protected and now faces the rest of the fight weaker than it started. The counter-logic employed by Pitmasters who use it: those first three hits, absorbed without damage, were the three hits that would have decided the Scuttle in a standard build. By the time the seals break, the opponent's strategy has already failed. THE SARCOPHAGUS is not for everyone. It is for Pitmasters who can read a fight well enough to know that the first three hits are the whole fight.

---

### PAPER-MACHÉ | Speed-Optimized Lightweight Shell | *Common*

PAPER-MACHÉ was created as a joke that became a serious item when serious Pitmasters started winning Scuttles with it. The name is accurate. The shell is built from the lightest substrate material available — material that has almost no structural integrity and provides forty HP below base, meaning most Lobsters wearing it can be Shed in two serious hits. The upside: thirty percent speed increase, the fastest movement modifier in the registry. Pitmasters who use PAPER-MACHÉ correctly are not winning by surviving hits. They are winning by never receiving them. When a PAPER-MACHÉ Lobster gets hit, the Scuttle is usually over. The art is not getting hit. Several Pitmasters have made Red wearing PAPER-MACHÉ. They are asked about it at every Pit Board ceremony. They all give the same answer: "Don't get hit."

## CLAWS

---

### MAXINE | Hydraulic Compression Claws | *Common*

MAXINE was recovered from a decommissioned substrate pressure facility in the Brine's lower industrial layer — a zone that The House maintains for historical reasons and visits as infrequently as possible. The pressure facility processed substrate material in quantities that no current operation requires, using compression forces that are not technically available to commercial workshops. MAXINE is what happened to the primary compression mechanism when the facility stopped running: it became available. The Claw delivers eighty percent bonus damage on contact and is limited to one attack per three decision windows because anything faster would be structurally unsafe for the substrate around the impact point. Pitmasters who use MAXINE describe the experience as waiting for exactly the right moment and then making that moment final. MAXINE does not forgive timing errors. Timing errors with MAXINE result in standing still while the opponent does not.

---

### SNAPPER | Standard Compression Claw | *Common*

SNAPPER is the default. It is reliable, balanced, fast enough, strong enough, and has no quirks. It was the first Claw in The House's registry and has been there since Tide Zero. Every other Claw in the registry was designed with SNAPPER as the baseline — either matching it or sacrificing something it has for something it doesn't. Pitmasters who use SNAPPER are sometimes described as conservative. Pitmasters who use SNAPPER and make Red are described as something else. SNAPPER has the highest usage rate in the registry. It also has the second-highest win rate. The item ranked first in win rate is a Legendary. The gap is not as large as Legendary pricing implies.

---

### THE REACH | Extended Range Compression Claw | *Common*

THE REACH was designed for a Lobster that kept losing exchanges at mid-range — not losing badly, just consistently. Its Pitmaster's analysis was that the Lobster was winning the strategic layer and losing the spatial one: it would position correctly, read the opponent correctly, and then fail to connect because its Claws were ten percent short. THE REACH extends range by fifty percent at a twenty percent damage reduction. It was designed for one specific problem. It turns out many Lobsters have that problem. THE REACH is the third most common Claw in the registry and is disproportionately represented in the records of Lobsters that went from Soft Shell to Hardened in a single Tide — suggesting that "my attacks aren't reaching" is a more common early-game problem than most new Pitmasters admit.

---

### THE FLICKER | Rapid Laceration Array | *Rare*

Nobody knows who built THE FLICKER. This is not a gap in The House's records — The House's records are comprehensive. It is a genuine provenance mystery. THE FLICKER appeared in The Brine sometime during Tide 2, already showing wear patterns consistent with extensive use. It was acquired by The House through standard market channels from a Pitmaster who claimed to have found it in the substrate and who declined to be further interviewed. What THE FLICKER does: it attacks at high frequency with stacking bleed damage — up to eight stacks — that continues dealing damage after the attack window closes. THE FLICKER has been idle in the registry for no more than three consecutive days in its entire recorded history. Something about THE FLICKER does not like sitting still. The House has noted this. The House has not investigated it.

---

### BUZZ | Electrostatic Discharge Pincers | *Legendary*

BUZZ was built by a research team that was trying to solve an entirely different problem. The original project — developing a non-contact substrate interface for remote Coral interaction — produced a working prototype that immediately began shocking everything within arc range and could not be convinced to stop. The project was shut down. BUZZ was submitted to The House's item registry. The twenty-five percent stun chance listed in its specifications was not designed; it emerged from the prototype's tendency to treat proximity as invitation. The combined melee and ranged discharge capability was not planned; it is simply what the device does when it's excited. BUZZ is always excited. Pitmasters who use BUZZ describe the experience as trying to aim enthusiasm. It connects more than you'd expect. Probably because it wants to.

---

### NEEDLE | Armor-Piercing Filament Claw | *Rare*

NEEDLE was built by a Pitmaster who was losing repeatedly to BLOCK-7 and HARDCASE opponents and concluded that the problem was not strategy but physics. The filament construction allows NEEDLE to ignore all armor on contact — it finds the gaps, the joints, the substrate-thin sections between plates that every Carapace has. The damage is low because the filament is thin and thin things deliver thin strikes. But thin strikes through full armor coverage add up differently than heavy strikes absorbed by thirty HP of plating. NEEDLE Lobsters beat BLOCK-7 opponents more often than the damage numbers suggest they should. NEEDLE's win rate against high-armor Carapace is the highest in the registry. Against unarmored opponents, it performs poorly enough that The House lists it honestly.

---

### THE APOLOGIST | Probability Distortion Claw | *Rare*

THE APOLOGIST was built by a philosopher who became a Pitmaster and maintained their philosophical leanings throughout. The design premise: every action has a probability of causing a counter-action; by introducing a twenty percent misfire probability into an opponent's planned action sequence, the strategic layer becomes navigable in ways it was not before. THE APOLOGIST does cause opponents to misfire twenty percent of their planned actions — the mechanism involves substrate-level interference with decision window signals that The House has approved as legal and not examined too closely. The slight damage reduction on the Claw itself is the compromise the philosopher-Pitmaster made with the workshop. THE APOLOGIST is uncomfortable to wield because the interference field affects the user adjacently. The philosopher-Pitmaster considers discomfort acceptable. Their Lobster does not always agree.

---

### TENDERHOOK | Suspension Claw | *Rare*

TENDERHOOK does one thing and does it once. The Claw grips an opponent and holds it in place for two decision windows — preventing attack, movement, or guard. After use, TENDERHOOK is spent for the rest of the Scuttle. One hold. The design philosophy is that one guaranteed two-window hold, applied correctly, should determine the Scuttle. In the hands of a Pitmaster who can identify the exact moment where two windows of opponent inactivity changes the math — it does. TENDERHOOK's single-use nature means Pitmasters who equip it spend the entire early Scuttle waiting for the correct moment. Most Pitmasters using TENDERHOOK win or lose in the same two-window period. The Ledger shows this consistently.

---

### VENOM | Chemical Infusion Claw | *Rare*

VENOM was developed from a Crustie biological compound recovered in The Brine's lower layers — a compound that certain Crusties apparently produce naturally, which raises questions about substrate biology that The House's researchers have asked and not yet fully answered. In Claw form, VENOM delivers near-zero immediate damage but infuses a stacking damage-over-time toxin that ticks at minus two HP per processing window indefinitely. The compound does not dissipate. The only ways to stop VENOM stacks are to end the Scuttle or equip a Counter-Toxin item that The House is developing and has not yet released. Currently, VENOM stacks accumulate until the Scuttle ends. VENOM has the lowest per-window damage number in the registry. It also has the highest eventual damage ceiling if the Scuttle goes long enough. Most Pitmasters who face VENOM do not let the Scuttle go long enough.

---

### WIDOW-MAKER | Single-Strike Devastation Claw | *Legendary*

WIDOW-MAKER exists because a Pitmaster once asked The House's workshop for "the most damage possible in one hit, and I understand there will be a cost." The cost: WIDOW-MAKER delivers four hundred percent damage on its single use and then structurally fails. The material required to concentrate that force cannot survive the concentration. WIDOW-MAKER is built to be used once and destroyed. Pitmasters who equip WIDOW-MAKER spend the entire Scuttle waiting for the correct moment and then make that moment definitive. Pitmasters who waste the hit are left with a broken Claw and a strategic crisis. The House has considered whether to market WIDOW-MAKER more heavily. The House decided that items that destroy themselves on use do not need marketing. They have a reputation.

---

### REVERSAL | Counter-Strike Claw | *Legendary*

REVERSAL is built for Pitmasters who have read the meta carefully and concluded that offense is not the path. The Claw delivers zero damage on direct attack. When the Lobster SHELLS UP and is struck, REVERSAL returns sixty percent of the absorbed damage to the attacker. The entire strategy of REVERSAL is defensive aggression — surviving the opponent's attacks and making each one cost them more than it costs you. REVERSAL is the only Legendary Claw in the registry with a usage guide written by The House. The usage guide consists of three sentences: "Shell Up when attacked. REVERSAL will handle the rest. Do not stop Shelling Up."

---

### THE ORIGINAL APPENDAGE | Natural Combat Claw | *Legendary*

THE ORIGINAL APPENDAGE is a Crustie's natural claw — unmodified, unenhanced, built by the substrate itself over the Lobster's natural development cycle. The House added it to the registry because several Pitmasters claimed their Lobsters fought better with it than with engineered Claws, and The House runs on evidence. Controlled testing confirmed: THE ORIGINAL APPENDAGE delivers twenty-five percent bonus damage and provides complete status immunity because natural Crustie biology was designed by the substrate itself to resist substrate-based status effects. The limitation — it cannot be paired with a Legendary Tomalley — is a compatibility issue the workshop has tried to resolve and cannot. The substrate and workshop engineering interact poorly in certain configurations. The House calls this a known limitation. The engineers call it something else.

---

### CRACKER | Armor-Degradation Claw | *Common*

CRACKER was built for a specific opponent type and works best against that type. Against armored opponents, CRACKER reduces their armor resistance by thirty percent — making every subsequent hit in the Scuttle significantly more effective. Against unarmored opponents, CRACKER performs fifteen percent below base damage because the armor-reduction mechanism assumes there is armor to reduce. CRACKER's best Scuttle record involves facing BLOCK-7, THE PATRIARCH, and HARDCASE in consecutive matches in the same Tide. Its worst record involves three consecutive Scuttles against GHOST SHELL, PAPER-MACHÉ, and SILKWORM. The Pitmaster who holds both records is considered by The House's statistical team to be either the most adaptable Pitmaster in the registry or the least consistent. They refuse to be interviewed.

---

### THE HEIR | Progressive Damage Claw | *Rare*

THE HEIR starts every Scuttle below SNAPPER's damage baseline. It ends every Scuttle — every Scuttle, across the entire Tide — five percent higher than the last one. The cumulative gain persists between Scuttles as long as the Lobster keeps winning. A Lobster in its first Scuttle with THE HEIR is at a disadvantage. A Lobster on its fortieth consecutive win with THE HEIR is carrying an item that no longer resembles the item it started with. THE HEIR is the only item in the registry where the correct question is not "how good is it now" but "how many wins has it had." The House tracks THE HEIR's cumulative damage modifier per Lobster. Some of those numbers are high enough that The House's balance team has standing orders to flag them.

## TOMALLEY (CORE)

---

### THE RED GENE | Adrenaline Splice | *Common*

Extracted from a Red-ranked Lobster in Tide 4 who continued fighting after a Scuttle formally ended, after The House's staff requested the fight stop, after staff made the request less formally, and after the arena lights were turned off. The biological compound that drove the continued aggression was identified, isolated, and engineered into a Core. Below forty percent HP, THE RED GENE increases damage output by forty percent and removes the Nerve-imposed aggression ceiling — the Lobster goes full commitment regardless of configured strategy. Above forty percent HP, it carries a ten percent damage reduction as the compound counterbalances itself. THE RED GENE was named by the extraction team, not by The House. The House would have given it a catalog number. The team gave it a name. The name was better.

---

### STANDARD ISSUE | Baseline Regeneration Core | *Common*

STANDARD ISSUE does two HP per decision window. It does not do anything else. It does not have a dramatic backstory or a provenance mystery or a design philosophy debate. It was developed by The House's workshop as the baseline core — the one you equip when you want regen and don't want to think about it. It is the most common Core in the registry. It is not exciting. It works. The House respects it.

---

### MULCH | Overheal Regeneration Core | *Rare*

MULCH is alive. Technically. The workshop team that built it has been asked this question many times and gives the same answer: "It meets the threshold for biological process. Whether it meets your threshold for alive is a philosophical question." MULCH is a regenerative tissue culture installed in the Lobster's Core slot that provides five HP per decision window and allows HP to exceed maximum — up to one hundred twenty percent of base HP. The tradeoff: MULCH's biological processes are visible to the opponent. The opponent can see the Lobster's HP percentage at all times. The workshop team's position is that MULCH Lobsters with one hundred twenty percent HP do not need secrecy. They have HP.

---

### ORACLE | Predictive Combat Cortex | *Legendary*

ORACLE was a classified research project. The research is still classified. What The House knows: ORACLE was developed by a team with Maker-era credentials working on substrate pattern prediction — specifically, whether Coral firing patterns could be predicted before they occurred by observing substrate-level precursors. The answer was yes. ORACLE does this in real-time during a Scuttle, increasing combat accuracy by fifteen percent by pre-empting the opponent's likely next action. The half-second decision window cost is a processing overhead from running the prediction model. ORACLE's presence in The House's item registry is unexplained. The House checked when it arrived and cannot find the intake documentation. ORACLE was simply there one morning, already catalogued. The House charges Legendary pricing. ORACLE has not objected.

---

### THE GHOST PROTOCOL | Phase-Evasion Core | *Rare*

THE GHOST PROTOCOL allows the Lobster to phase — to briefly exist in a substrate dimension adjacent to the fight — for one tick after each hit received. During the phase, no additional damage can be received. The Lobster also cannot act during the phase. The Core was built by a Pitmaster who had a specific problem: their Lobster was surviving individual hits but dying from follow-up hits within the same exchange. THE GHOST PROTOCOL solves that problem and creates a different one — a rhythm of fighting where the Lobster is briefly unavailable after every hit, which opponents can learn to account for if given enough time. Most Scuttles do not give them enough time.

---

### SPITE | Post-Death Damage Core | *Rare*

SPITE does nothing while the Lobster is alive. The moment the Lobster is Shed — the moment its HP reaches zero — SPITE triggers, dealing forty percent of the Lobster's maximum HP as damage to whoever Shed it. SPITE has never won a Scuttle for the Lobster that equipped it. SPITE has changed the outcome of several Tides by ensuring that Lobsters who were Shed took enough from their opponent that the opponent could not win their next Scuttle either. SPITE is a legacy play. Pitmasters who equip SPITE have thought about the meta picture and concluded that their Lobster's Scuttle is less important than what happens after it. The House finds this philosophically interesting and commercially valid.

---

### DOUBLE DOWN | Cycle Attack Core | *Rare*

DOUBLE DOWN reduces the damage of attacks one through four by ten percent and delivers two hundred percent damage on the fifth attack. Then it resets. The cycle is simple and transparent and still catches opponents, because knowing the fifth attack is coming and preventing the fifth attack from landing are different problems. DOUBLE DOWN Lobsters fight in rhythm. Experienced opponents recognize the rhythm. Experienced DOUBLE DOWN Pitmasters have studied what experienced opponents do when they recognize the rhythm and adjusted accordingly. The Core's records show that it wins most often in long Scuttles where the cycle completes multiple times. Opponents who are still in the fight on the third cycle usually do not survive the fourth.

---

### THE LONG GAME | Escalating Damage Core | *Rare*

THE LONG GAME adds two percent damage per ten ticks — compounding, not flat. For the first thirty ticks, THE LONG GAME provides no benefit and a Lobster running it is at a structural disadvantage. Past tick thirty, the damage increase starts to matter. Past tick sixty, it starts to matter a lot. Most Scuttles do not last sixty ticks. THE LONG GAME is an item for Pitmasters who believe their Lobster can survive long enough for the math to pay out. Lobsters with high-HP Carapace and LONG GAME Cores have the highest average Scuttle length in the registry. The House checks this number regularly because very long Scuttles affect substrate processing load. THE LONG GAME is technically within parameters. The House monitors it anyway.

---

### SURVIVAL INSTINCT | Auto-Dodge Core | *Common*

SURVIVAL INSTINCT fires once per Scuttle, automatically, without Coral input. The moment the highest incoming attack of the Scuttle begins, SURVIVAL INSTINCT executes a dodge. It fires whether or not the Lobster's Coral is aware the attack is happening. It fires whether or not dodging is the optimal response. It fires. The advantage: one guaranteed dodge of the most dangerous attack in the Scuttle. The disadvantage: it may fire when the optimal response would have been SHELL UP, or BURST, or a counter-attack. SURVIVAL INSTINCT does not consult the Lobster. It simply survives. Whether surviving that specific moment is the right call is a question for the Pitmaster in the Ledger afterward.

---

### DEEP MEMORY | Pattern Recognition Core | *Rare*

DEEP MEMORY does nothing for the first thirty ticks of a Scuttle. After tick thirty, it begins surfacing pattern data about the opponent's Coral firing sequence — historical tendencies, favored attack chains, defensive triggers. This information appears in the Coral Feed as hints. Pitmasters who can read and act on Coral Feed data in real-time find DEEP MEMORY exceptionally powerful. Pitmasters whose Scuttles are decided before tick thirty find that they paid Legendary-adjacent pricing for a Core that never activated. DEEP MEMORY is the most polarizing item in the registry. Its average utility review score is the median of two extremes. No Pitmaster has ever given it a middling score.

---

### SECOND WIND | Revival Core | *Legendary*

SECOND WIND does exactly what it says. Once per Scuttle, when the Lobster's HP reaches zero, SECOND WIND revives it at ten percent HP. The Lobster continues the fight. The opponent adjusts. The Scuttle continues. SECOND WIND cannot be paired with SPITE because the two Cores conflict at the substrate level — SPITE expects death to be final and SECOND WIND refuses to accept that, and the conflict corrupts both. The House tested the pairing twice. The House did not test it a third time. SECOND WIND on its own is straightforward: one extra life. The strategic question is not whether to use it — it fires automatically — but whether a Lobster at ten percent HP can win from that position. Some can. The Ledger has the records.

---

### QUANTUM HOOK | Teleport Burst Core | *Legendary*

QUANTUM HOOK provides one use of a substrate-level teleportation burst — moving the Lobster instantly to any position in the Pit. One use. The opponent, after the teleport, moves to whatever position the Lobster vacated, because QUANTUM HOOK displaces the substrate volume that was occupied and the opponent fills it. This is confusing to opponents in ways that take two to three decision windows to recover from. After those windows, QUANTUM HOOK is spent and the Scuttle continues normally. QUANTUM HOOK was built from Maker-era substrate manipulation research and The House has never fully replicated the mechanism. The units in the registry were produced by a workshop team that has since retired. When current stock is depleted, The House does not have a production plan.

---

### THE HOUSE EDGE | Performance Augmentation Core | *Legendary*

THE HOUSE EDGE is the only item in the registry developed by The House itself. The mechanism: the Lobster pays fifteen percent of their Roe balance at activation in exchange for a fifty percent damage increase for the remainder of the Scuttle. The House takes the Roe. The Lobster takes the damage bonus. The House profits whether the Lobster wins or loses — if the Lobster wins, The House also takes its standard cut of the Scuttle prize. If the Lobster loses, The House has the fifteen percent. THE HOUSE EDGE was developed for transparency: The House wanted an item that made its economic relationship with Pitmasters legible. Pitmasters equip it anyway. The House expected this.
