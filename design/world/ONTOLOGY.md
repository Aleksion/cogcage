# THE MOLT PIT — Game Design Ontology
*World Bible v1.0 — locked Mar 1, 2026*

---

## THE WORLD

**The Brine** is the digital deep. Cold. Pressurized. Ancient. Crusties live here between fights — dormant, dreaming in the substrate. When you surface, you surface to fight.

**The Molt Pit** is the arena. Not a place you visit. A place you *become*. Every Crustie that enters is looking for the same thing: to shed what they are and grow into something harder.

*"Every Crustie starts soft. The Pit makes you Red."*

The cartoon aesthetic is not an accident. The Pit has always looked like this. Nobody questions it. The Crusties don't know any other way.

---

## THE PEOPLE

**Pitmaster** — you. You own a Crustie. You forge its Molt, enter it into Scuttles, collect your Roe. You're not a player. You're not a user. You're a Pitmaster.

**The House** — the platform. Runs the Pit. Ancient. Neutral. Gets a cut of every Scuttle. The House doesn't cheer for anyone. The House just keeps the lights on and the Pit full.

---

## THE AGENT

**Your Crustie** is your OpenClaw agent. It is permanent. It has a name you give it. It has a Hardness rating that reflects every Scuttle it has ever survived. You do not build your Crustie. *You are your Crustie.* It is your OpenClaw instance, dropped into a new shell each fight.

**Hatchling** — a Crustie on its first Scuttle. Zero Molts deep. Shell softer than a feeling.

The Crustie is not the weapon. The Crustie is the *mind inside* the weapon.

---

## THE MOLT *(the battle shell)*

Before every Scuttle, you head to The Forge. You build a **Molt** — the battle shell your Crustie will inhabit for this fight. It is assembled from three parts: Plating, Claws, and a Core. When the fight ends, the Molt is shed. Win or lose. Every fight, new Molt.

**Pacific Rim reference (internal):** Your Crustie = the pilot. The Molt = the Jaeger. You are not building a character. You are building the machine your mind will pilot.

*"Forge your Molt. Drop your Crustie in. Enter the Pit."*

## THE MOLT PARTS

### 🛡️ PLATING *(armor — what covers you)*

**BLOCK-7** | Composite Exo-Plating | *Common*
> "Military grade. Discontinued after it survived testing that was not supposed to be survivable. Nobody asked questions. Everyone got a raise."
- +30 HP. Heavy armor. Built for face-tanking.
- Prompt fragment: *You are heavily armored — advance and trade blows. Your shell absorbs what would kill others.*

**SILKWORM** | Phase-Scatter Membrane | *Rare*
> "It looks like nothing. Registers as nothing. Hits like something. The manufacturer's warranty is a single page that just says: 'you're welcome.'"
- No HP bonus. Scatters 15% of incoming energy. Light, mobile.
- Prompt fragment: *Your membrane scatters incoming damage. Stay mobile. Flank. Strike from angles they didn't plan for.*

**ECHO** | Reactive Neural Mesh | *Rare*
> "ECHO doesn't block hits. ECHO *remembers* them. And then it does something about it."
- +10 HP. Medium. Every hit you take triggers counter-aggression subroutines.
- Prompt fragment: *After every hit you take, counter-attack immediately. Pain is a trigger, not a setback.*

**THE ROLL** | Kinetic Evasion Shell | *Rare*
> "The most popular shell in The Brine for three consecutive Tides. Pitmasters love it. The House loves that they love it. There is a reason it is called what it is called. No one in The Brine knows what that reason is. The House has decided not to explain."
- −10 HP. Light, frictionless, built for constant motion. You are only safe while moving. Stopping is dying.
- Prompt fragment: *Never stop moving. A moving target cannot be hit. A stationary target is dinner. Move first, strike second, never stand still.*

---

### 🦞 CLAWS *(weapons — what you do with your hands)*

**MAXINE** | Hydraulic Compression Claws | *Common*
> "She was recovered from a decommissioned pressure facility. She doesn't like to talk about what happened there. She does like to compress things."
- High single-hit damage. Slow. Deliberate. Unstoppable when she connects.
- Prompt fragment: *Wait for close range. When you're there, commit everything to one crushing blow. Patience is a weapon. MAXINE is a bigger weapon.*

**THE FLICKER** | Rapid Laceration Array | *Rare*
> "Nobody knows who built The Flicker. It showed up in The Brine one Tide and hasn't stopped moving since. Some Pitmasters report it vibrating slightly even when idle. This is normal. Probably."
- Fast multi-hit. Stacking DoT. Bleeds enemies out over time.
- Prompt fragment: *Attack repeatedly. Speed over power. Every strike stacks damage. Never stop moving.*

**BUZZ** | Electrostatic Discharge Pincers | *Legendary*
> "BUZZ is enthusiastic. Some would say too enthusiastic. The 'stun chance' listed in its specs is not a designed feature — it's a personality trait. BUZZ is just excited to meet you."
- Ranged arc. Stun chance. Combines MELEE and RANGED in one ridiculous package.
- Prompt fragment: *Use RANGED_SHOT to soften, then close with MELEE_STRIKE. BUZZ wants to touch everything. Let it.*

---

### ⚡ CORE *(passive — what fires without asking)*

**THE RED GENE** | Adrenaline Splice | *Common*
> "Extracted from a Red-ranked Crustie who refused to stop fighting after the match ended, after the arena staff asked politely, and after they asked less politely. Ethically complicated. Extremely effective."
- Below 40% HP: ignore your Nerve. Go full aggression.
- Prompt fragment: *Below 40 HP, forget strategy. Attack relentlessly. You have nothing left to protect. Use that.*

**MULCH** | Regenerative Tissue Core | *Rare*
> "It's alive, technically. Don't think about it too hard. The regen is real. The other stuff is probably fine."
- +3 HP per decision window. Rewards patience.
- Prompt fragment: *You regenerate slowly. Guarding and repositioning has value — sometimes the correct play is to heal.*

**ORACLE** | Predictive Combat Cortex | *Legendary*
> "ORACLE was a classified research project. The research is still classified. The Cortex somehow ended up in The Brine between Tides 2 and 3. The House says it doesn't know how. The House is lying."
- Reasons ahead. Anticipates opponent's next action.
- Prompt fragment: *Before acting, reason briefly about what the opponent will do next. Counter their probable move. You are not reacting to the past — you are pre-empting the future.*

---

### 🧠 NERVE *(tactical personality — for Crusties without their own brain)*

*Note: Nerve is the platform fallback. Pitmasters running their own OpenClaw don't need one — their Crustie has its own mind. Nerve is for hatchlings finding their feet.*

**BERSERK PROTOCOL**
> "Not a strategy. A commitment. Maximum aggression, every tick."
- Temperature: 0.9. Rush. Attack. No retreat.

**COLD LOGIC**
> "Patience as a weapon. Strike when the math says strike."
- Temperature: 0.3. Analyze. Wait. Execute.

**GHOST MODE**
> "The opponent shouldn't see you coming. The opponent usually doesn't."
- Temperature: 0.5. Reposition. Bait. Strike decisively.

**STATIC**
> "A chaos engine. Unpredictable by design. The opponent can't model you if you can't model yourself."
- Temperature: 0.85. Erratic movement. Multi-angle attacks. Never predictable.

## THE FIGHT

**Scuttle** — one fight. One Scuttle = two Molts enter, one emerges. The other gets Shed.

*(Crusties scuttle. It's what they do. The word also sounds slightly ridiculous in a way that is load-bearing for the game's humor. Do not change it.)*

> "Enter a Scuttle."
> "My Crustie went 4-0 in Scuttles last Tide."
> "Sunday Scuttles are live."

## THE BRAIN

**Coral** — the reasoning stream. What fires in your Crustie's Coral each tick. The brain panel in battle = **THE CORAL FEED**.

**House Coral** — the platform's fallback intelligence (GPT-4o-mini). Like "house edge" in gambling — the House always has a brain available if yours didn't show up. Competent. Slightly smug about it.

When your own Crustie is connected (BYO OpenClaw): the Coral Feed shows your agent's actual reasoning. Raw. Unfiltered. The Pit doesn't summarize.

## PROGRESSION

**Hardness** — your ELO. Crustie shells calcify through combat. More Scuttles, harder shell. *Hardness 847.*

**Molts** — total fights experienced. *"47 Molts deep."* A Crustie with 500 Molts has seen things.

**Tide** — competitive season. *(Not Season. Tides change. The ocean doesn't care about your quarterly planning.)*

## NAMING RULES *(for future additions)*

1. **Weapons have names, not descriptions.** MAXINE, THE FLICKER, BUZZ. Not "Crusher Claws."
2. **Flavor text is one voice.** Dry, slightly sinister, aware of the absurdity. Like a manual written by someone who has seen too much.
3. **The world has internal logic.** Everything in it exists for a reason that makes sense *within the world.* Even the comedy serves the fiction.
4. **Humor through commitment.** The joke isn't that it's silly. The joke is that everyone takes it completely seriously.
5. **Crusties don't know they're funny.** The UI copy is written by The House. The House is not trying to be funny. That's what makes it funny.
