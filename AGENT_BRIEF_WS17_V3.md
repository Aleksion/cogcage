## THE GAME — WHAT IS ACTUALLY HAPPENING

This is an AI battle arena. Here is what mechanically exists:

- **Crusties** — crustacean creatures. Five species: lobster-type, crab-type, mantis-type, hermit-type, shrimp-type. They fight. They molt. They grow harder. The molt is real — old shell shed, new shell grown, the Crustie inside changed by the process.
- **Chefs** — the humans. They don't fight. They *build* the thing their Crustie fights inside. They are engineers. Their Crustie is the driver. The Molt is the car.
- **The Molt** — the battle shell assembled before each fight. Three parts: **Carapace** (armor), **Claws** (weapons), **Tomalley** (passive organ that fires without asking). One fight, then shed. Every fight, new Molt.
- **The Scuttle** — the fight. Two Molts enter. One gets Shed. The word is slightly ridiculous. This is load-bearing.
- **The Shed** — where Molts are assembled. The build screen. A workspace.
- **Roe** — the currency. Crustie eggs. The absurdity is not acknowledged by anyone.
- **The Pit** — the arena. Where Scuttles happen.
- **Hardness** — the rank stat. Accumulates in The Pit. Nowhere else.
- **Red** — the highest designation. Not a rank. A state of being. Through the fire. Came out harder.
- **The Ledger** — the match history. Everything recorded. Nothing editable.
- **The Pit Board** — leaderboard. Soft Shell to Red.

The items — MAXINE, THE FLICKER, BUZZ, THE APOLOGIST, BLOCK-7, WIDOW, ORACLE, etc. — these exist. Their individual lore is already strong. You are not rewriting item lore. You are building the world those items exist inside.

## THE REGISTER

**High on Life meets Borderlands.** You know what this means. Commit to the bit. Dry. Matter-of-fact about things that should not be matter-of-fact. The world has internal logic and that logic runs to its conclusion without flinching.

The humor is never "isn't this absurd." The humor is the world being completely serious about things that are completely absurd. The Crusties don't know they're in a game. The Pit doesn't care about your feelings. The platform entity — whatever it is — is doing its job with full professional compliance and zero awareness that the job is strange.

**Reference points:**
- High on Life: every NPC has a fully-realized inner life that has nothing to do with you. The alien invasion broadcast explains its logic for four minutes straight and every explanation makes it worse.
- Borderlands: JAKOBS doesn't make guns, JAKOBS makes *heirlooms*. TEDIORE guns are thrown like grenades because that costs less than reloading. The manufacturer philosophy IS the joke.
- The items already written: MAXINE doesn't consider herself a weapon. THE APOLOGIST says sorry every time. STANDARD ISSUE has no personality and the company that made it dissolved immediately after shipping it.

That register. Match it. For the whole world.

## WHAT YOU ARE BUILDING

### 1. `design/world/LORE.md` — FULL REWRITE

Answer these questions. Answer them in the voice of someone who finds these questions mildly annoying because the answers are so obvious, and is explaining them anyway because someone apparently needs them explained.

**What is The Brine?**
Not "ancient substrate." Give it a *reason* to exist. The funniest reason. The reason that, when you hear it, makes everything else make more sense and also funnier. The Brine is the world. What kind of world? Why is it called The Brine? What lives in it besides Crusties?

**What are Crusties?**
Five species. They molt. They fight. They are, against all odds, developing something that might be called intelligence, in the same way that something left in a petri dish for long enough develops something that might be called culture. What does that look like? What do Crusties want, when they're not in The Pit? Do they want anything? Does The Pit make them want things they didn't before?

**What are Chefs?**
They don't fight. They engineer. They have names for their Crusties. They have opinions about loadouts. They lose sleep over TOMALLEY configurations. What kind of person becomes a Chef? What is the relationship between a Chef and their Crustie, honestly? Not the noble version. The real version.

**What runs The Pit?**
This is the big question. Replace "The House" or rebuild it from scratch. Whatever runs The Pit needs to be: ancient (or at least established), self-interested in ways it doesn't fully acknowledge, the source of all UI copy (every loading screen, every error message, every flavor line — this entity wrote it), and *funny in a specific way*. The casino-that-doesn't-know-it's-a-casino framing is available if you want it. So is everything else.

**What is The Pit, really?**
Not mechanically. Emotionally. Why does The Pit exist? Not the practical answer. The real answer — the one that explains why Crusties come back after being Shed, why Chefs lose sleep over configurations, why Red matters even though nothing changes when you get there except everything.

**What happened to Subject 1?**
The first Red. The first anything to reach Red. Three sentences max. Leave something unsaid.

**What is The Deep?**
Below The Pit. Nobody maps it. Something is there. What?

Minimum 1500 words. This should read like it was written by the world, not about it.

### 2. `design/ui/SOFT-SHELL-GUIDE.md` — FULL REWRITE

The onboarding. Never call it a tutorial. Written by whatever runs The Pit.

**The bit:** Whatever runs The Pit is legally, contractually, or operationally required to provide orientation to new Chefs. It is doing so. Fully. Professionally. With complete compliance and something that is almost, but not quite, suppressed exasperation. By the end, the new Chef should feel like they've been briefed by something that knows considerably more than it's saying, and has made a professional decision not to say it, and is very comfortable with that decision.

Must end with: *"The Pit is ready. Your Crustie is waiting. You can read more later. Or you can enter now."*
