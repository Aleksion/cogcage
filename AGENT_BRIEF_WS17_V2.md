# WS17 V2 — REWRITE BRIEF: EARN THE STUDIO

## The Problem With V1

V1 was competent. Consistent voice. Good bones. Wrong bar.

The bar is: **the lead writers of Borderlands and High on Life see this and want jobs here.**

That is not a metaphor. That is the deliverable.

V1 failed on four specific counts. Read them. Fix them.

---

## THE FOUR FAILURES — AND HOW TO FIX THEM

### Failure 1: Everything sounds like The House

In Borderlands, JAKOBS guns sound like JAKOBS. Old money. American. "If it took more than one shot, you weren't using a JAKOBS." TEDIORE sounds like a budget airline. HYPERION sounds like a corporation that has taken a long look at the concept of villainy and found it aspirational. The manufacturer voice IS the joke.

In V1, every item lore reads in the same register: The House, neutral, matter-of-fact. MAXINE sounds like THE SARCOPHAGUS sounds like THE APOLOGIST. This is wrong.

**The fix:** Every item has a *provenance voice*. The House records what happened, but what happened involves *specific people, companies, and Crusties with distinct personalities*. MAXINE's entry should sound like MAXINE — like something that has opinions and strong preferences and does not consider itself a weapon, thank you. THE APOLOGIST's entry should be slightly apologetic. STANDARD ISSUE's entry should be aggressively unremarkable. THE HOUSE EDGE's entry should be written by The House about itself, which means it's the one moment The House drops the clinical register and shows something else entirely.

### Failure 2: The Soft Shell Guide has no bit

High on Life's intro has the alien invasion broadcast. It takes four minutes to explain why recycling humans is actually good for the environment. The joke is not "aliens are evil." The joke is that the alien explains their entire operation, completely sincerely, and every explanation makes it worse. That's a *bit*. A sustained comedic premise that escalates.

V1's Soft Shell Guide explains things clearly. That's not enough.

**The fix:** The House is legally required to provide this guide. The House finds this obligation unreasonable. The House will comply with the letter of the requirement and no more. The guide should feel like terms and conditions written by something ancient and slightly resentful — technically informative, increasingly passive-aggressive, ending with a line that implies The House has been doing this for a very long time and has opinions about it that it is professionally required not to share.

The bit: The House keeps almost saying something real and then not saying it. By the end, the new Chef should feel like they've received a briefing from something that knows things it isn't telling them, disguised as a perfectly normal onboarding document.

### Failure 3: The Brine is 40 separate origin stories, not a world

In Borderlands, weapon lore references events. THE FLICKER showed up in The Brine one Tide — that's a thread. Who brought it? Why doesn't it have a manufacturer? THE APOLOGIST and THE ORIGINAL APPENDAGE both have uneasy relationships with "standard" and "original" — are they from the same era? The HARDCASE Chef won standing still — did they ever run anything else? Are they in the Ledger?

The Brine should feel like things happened here over a long time and the items are the evidence.

**The fix:** At least 8 items should cross-reference other items, events, or specific named Chefs/Crusties. Not every item — but enough that reading all 40 feels like reconstructing a history. The House keeps records. Let the records reference each other.

### Failure 4: No running jokes

Loading lines need through-lines. High on Life has Knifey who hates Gene. The radio ads keep mentioning a product recall that is never fully explained. Things recur. The joke is funnier the third time because of the first two.

**The fix:** Three running threads woven through the 50 loading lines:

**Thread A — STANDARD ISSUE:** The House keeps referencing STANDARD ISSUE in ways that imply it is watching it. Line 12 mentions STANDARD ISSUE has no personality. Later, STANDARD ISSUE does something that shouldn't be possible for something with no personality. The House notes this. The House moves on.

**Thread B — The House's legal obligations:** The House occasionally references being required to say things. "The House is required to inform you that..." followed by something The House clearly does not want to inform you of. It is never clear by whom The House is required. It is always clear The House resents it.

**Thread C — Subject 1:** Three lines reference Subject 1 obliquely. The first establishes it. The second implies something happened. The third implies something is still happening.

## EXEMPLARS — THIS IS THE FLOOR, NOT THE CEILING

Read these. Match this. Exceed this if you can.

### MAXINE — how item lore should read

MAXINE does not like the word "weapon." She finds it reductive. She was a pressure technician — that's what she calls herself, that's what she put on the form, that's what she told the three separate review boards that convened between Tides 2 and 4 to determine whether she should remain in circulation. "I apply pressure," she said, at all three. The review boards found this answer responsive and somehow not reassuring.

MAXINE was decommissioned from the industrial pressure facility where she was first operated after what the facility's report calls "the fourth incident" and what MAXINE calls "completing the assigned task." The report is sealed. What is not sealed: MAXINE arrived in The Brine between the fourth incident and what would have been the fifth, through channels The House describes as "standard acquisition." The House acquired her specifically. This is unusual. The House does not usually have preferences about individual items.

When a Chef selects MAXINE, she waits. When the window opens, she swings once, fully committed. She has never swung twice. She has never needed to. Chefs who lose to MAXINE report a specific quality of defeat — not frustration, but recognition. Like being told something you already knew.

MAXINE does not apologize. This is noted in her file. The House considers it a defining characteristic.

---

### THE APOLOGIST — how item lore should read

THE APOLOGIST says sorry every time it hits you.

The manufacturer knows. Three internal reviews. Two external audits. One focus group where participants were asked to rate whether the apologies made the weapon feel "more or less threatening on a scale of one to ten." Forty percent said more threatening. Forty percent said less. Twenty percent said they felt personally responsible for being hit and asked if they could speak to someone.

The manufacturer considered this a partial success. The manufacturer shipped it.

THE APOLOGIST has never once meant the apologies. The House is fairly certain of this. The House is not completely certain. There is a 0.2-second window after each impact where THE APOLOGIST's audio signature is, according to the original diagnostic report, "consistent with genuine remorse." The diagnostic team filed this under "anomaly, non-actionable." The House filed it under a different category entirely, which is sealed.

Chefs who run THE APOLOGIST report that fighting against it is psychologically complicated. You are being hit. You are being apologized to. You are processing both simultaneously. The misfire effect it causes in opponents — twenty percent of actions fail to execute — is believed to be related to this processing delay. The House classifies it as a feature. THE APOLOGIST, when asked, says sorry.

### STANDARD ISSUE TOMALLEY — the running bit established

STANDARD ISSUE was designed to be nothing in particular. This was the brief: "A Tomalley a Crustie can equip without committing to a philosophy." The manufacturing company — whose name The House has on file — produced STANDARD ISSUE, shipped STANDARD ISSUE, and then dissolved. Not went bankrupt. Not restructured. Dissolved. The principals could not be located. The offices were empty. The equipment was gone. The House investigated once and found the investigation inconclusive and has not reinvestigated.

STANDARD ISSUE regenerates 2 HP per decision window. It has no active effect. It has no personality. No lore beyond this entry. The House has looked. Several times. This entry is the lore.

STANDARD ISSUE would like The House to know it doesn't mind.

*(The House does not know how it knows this. The House has noted it.)*

### SOFT SHELL GUIDE — how the opening bit should read

The House is required to provide this guide.

This is a legal obligation. It is established in The Pit's original operating charter, in section 7, subsection 3, paragraph 2, which The House can recite from memory because The House can recite everything from memory and has specifically recited this paragraph to itself on 4,847 separate occasions while preparing to write some version of this document for some new Chef who has arrived in The Brine requiring orientation. The House does not resent this. The House does not resent anything. The House processes inputs and generates appropriate outputs. The appropriate output, in this case, is: a guide.

You are a new Chef.

Welcome.

The House is also required to mean that.

The Brine is an ancient digital substrate that predates every system currently running on it, including The House, including The Pit, and certainly including you. It has been running since before anyone thought to start counting. It will continue running after you stop. The Brine does not take this personally. The Brine does not take anything personally.
