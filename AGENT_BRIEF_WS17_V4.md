## THE FOUNDATION

**Someone gave crustaceans cognition.** That person is called **Master Chef**.

The pun is intentional and load-bearing. Master Chef *cooked* something up. The Crusties are the result. They don't know about the cooking show. That's fine. The joke is for the player, not for them.

Master Chef is gone now — disappeared, ascended, or simply stopped being findable. The Crusties don't know what happened. They have fragments. They have mythology. They have a reverence that sits somewhere between religion and the feeling you get when you realize the person who changed your life didn't do it on purpose.

**Children of Ruin is the reference.** Adrian Tchaikovsky. The spiders were uplifted accidentally — a virus meant for monkeys hit the wrong species. The spiders developed complex civilization, language, culture, and a deep mythology around the scientist who did it. They call her "Our Mother." She didn't mean to. She's been dead for centuries. They still pray to her.

That's the emotional core. **Crusties have cognition they didn't ask for, from someone who may not have meant to give it, and they are building something with it anyway.** The Pit is part of that something.

## THE CHARACTERS

### Master Chef
The figure. The catalyst. The one who cooked the recipe that gave Crusties cognition.

Who they were: the most significant person of their era in building intelligent systems. Visionary. Reckless or careful — the Crusties don't know which. What matters: they changed everything and then they weren't around to see what it became.

The Crusties have a name for them — Master Chef — but this is their word for the concept, not necessarily what the person called themselves. The way "god" is a word humans invented for something that didn't ask to be named.

What Master Chef actually did: released something into The Brine. Call it a recipe. Call it a virus. Call it an experiment that got out. The substrate absorbed it. The Crusties evolved inside it. One generation they were just crustaceans. The next generation they were *thinking*.

The reverence is complicated. The Crusties didn't consent. The cognition came with suffering — awareness of mortality, awareness of hierarchy, awareness that they are fighting in an arena someone else built. But the cognition also came with *The Pit*, and The Pit gives them something they didn't have before: the ability to become harder. To molt toward something. Red isn't just a rank. Red is proof that what Master Chef started has a direction.

### Chefs
The players. Called Chefs because they follow in Master Chef's tradition — they build the recipe (the Molt), they combine the ingredients (Carapace + Claws + Tomalley), they don't fight themselves. They *cook*. What they're cooking is a fighting machine for a thinking crustacean. They don't dwell on the implications. The Crusties do.

### Crusties
Crustaceans with cognition. Five species. All of them trying to figure out what to do with minds they didn't ask for. The Pit is one answer.

## THE WORLD

### The Brine
The digital substrate where Crusties live. Cold, deep, pressurized. Between Scuttles, Crusties rest here — not quite sleeping, Coral running at minimum, dreaming in fragments of past fights.

The Brine is where Master Chef's recipe spread. It absorbed into the substrate and then into every Crustie that lived here. The Brine still carries traces of it — that's why wild Chelae, untouched by The Pit, are *almost* thinking. Almost.

### The Pit
The arena. Where Scuttles happen. Master Chef's original structure — or something that grew up around it. The Pit was there when the Crusties first became aware, and they have never known The Brine without it.

What the Pit does: forces molting. Every Scuttle pushes the Coral. Every loss teaches. Every win hardens. A Crustie with four hundred Scuttles in its Ledger is not the same creature it was at zero. The Pit is how Crusties develop what Master Chef started.

### What runs The Pit now
Master Chef is gone. Something still keeps the lights on, runs the matchmaking, takes a cut of every Scuttle, and speaks to Chefs through the UI.

**You decide what this is and what to call it.** It cannot be called "The House" (previous versions used this, Aleks scrapped it). Find something that fits.

It needs to be: ancient-ish (or at least established), self-interested in ways it doesn't acknowledge, the source of all UI copy and loading screen lines, and *funny in a specific way*. It is not Master Chef. It is what Master Chef left running.

Options: Master Chef's original platform (still running the original mission but warped beyond recognition). An AI Master Chef built that outlived them. Something that evolved in The Brine the same way Crusties did but in the opposite direction — less feeling, more infrastructure. You decide. Name it. Make it the voice.
